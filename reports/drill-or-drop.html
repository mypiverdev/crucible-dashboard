1. Executive Summary

Drill or Drop is an AI-powered well investment decision platform designed for non-operated working interest (NOWI) holders evaluating Authorization for Expenditure (AFE) proposals. The platform ingests AFE documents, validates land records against internal and third-party data, builds type curves from analog well performance data, auto-calculates full-cycle economics through an engine-driven model, and delivers a drill/drop recommendation through a comprehensive, interactive executive dashboard with live financial modeling, structured advisory voting, and a delegated approval chain with escalation.

The product addresses a fundamental challenge in upstream oil & gas: the AFE evaluation process for non-operators is manual, fragmented across spreadsheets and disconnected tools, and heavily dependent on institutional knowledge. Decisions that commit capital are often made without rigorous, repeatable analysis — or the analysis takes so long that the election window closes before work is complete.

Value Proposition: Drill or Drop compresses the AFE evaluation workflow from days or weeks into same-day automated results. It automates the mechanics (PDF parsing, land record matching, analog selection, economics calculations) while preserving expert control at every stage through role-based review gates with confidence-level sign-offs.

Key Architectural Change: Land and Technical Analysts work in parallel after AFE ingestion, eliminating the sequential bottleneck. Once both sign off, the economics engine auto-calculates the full model. The Commercial Analyst validates and adjusts rather than building from scratch. The dashboard is a live financial model — CAG members change inputs and watch everything recalculate in real time before casting votes.

If the election deadline passes without an approval decision, the system flags the AFE as Non-Consent and records the outcome in the decision archive.

The platform targets a client managing non-operated interests across the Permian (Delaware and Midland sub-basins), Eagle Ford, Haynesville, and Powder River basins. The MVP handles one AFE at a time. Well data is ingested from a multi-source strategy: free state regulatory data from Texas RRC, New Mexico OCD, and Wyoming WOGCC serve as the primary foundation, with Enverus CSV import as enrichment and fallback (especially for Louisiana/Haynesville data). The UI follows a light theme, modern, clean, professional design and is mobile-responsive from day one.

v2.1 Updates: This revision incorporates client decisions on 12 BA optimization recommendations (8 approved for v1, 1 for v2, 2 deferred, 1 skipped) and a state regulatory data strategy leveraging free public data as the primary foundation. New v1 features include: confidence auto-gating, deadline-aware prioritization, breakeven auto-calculation, asymmetric risk framing, stale evaluation detection, PDF template library, export package, and decision outcome data model. Timeline extended to 20 weeks.

2. Problem Statement

The Non-Operator's Dilemma

Non-operated working interest holders receive AFE proposals from operators and must make consequential capital allocation decisions — often under tight election deadlines (typically 30 days, worst case 1 week) — with limited access to the operator's subsurface data, completion design rationale, or economic assumptions.

Fragmented Data and Tooling

Land records live in Excel. Well data is pulled from Enverus manually via CSV exports. Economics are modeled in standalone spreadsheets. Commodity prices are looked up by hand. There is no single system that connects the evaluation pipeline end to end.

Manual, Error-Prone Workflows

DOI validation requires cross-referencing internal records against operator statements and public data. Analog well selection is subjective and inconsistent. Type curve generation depends on who is doing the work and what wells they happen to choose.

Slow Turnaround

A thorough AFE evaluation can take days to weeks depending on staff availability. When election windows are tight, delays translate directly into missed opportunities or uninformed decisions.

No Institutional Memory

Decisions are documented inconsistently. There is no structured archive of past evaluations, the assumptions that drove them, or how actual well performance compared to projections.

No Structured Advisory Input

Advisory group opinions are collected informally (email, hallway conversations, meetings) with no structured voting, no mandatory rationale capture, and no systematic way to aggregate sentiment for the decision-maker.

Approval Bottlenecks

The CEO or primary decision-maker is frequently traveling or unavailable. There is no systematic delegation chain, no automated escalation, and no way to act on approvals via SMS when away from a desk. Missed deadlines result in non-consent elections with potential financial penalties.

Limited Scenario Flexibility

Traditional spreadsheet models make it cumbersome to explore alternative deal structures. Each scenario requires manual rework, discouraging thorough exploration of the decision space.

Expert Bottlenecks

Senior technical and commercial staff are the scarce resource. They need tools that surface the analysis, not tools that require them to build it from scratch every time.

5. Workflow & Process Parallel Model

The v2.0 workflow is structured around a parallel processing model where Land and Technical Analysts work simultaneously after AFE ingestion. The economics engine auto-calculates the full model once both sign off. The CAG reviews and votes, then the Approver decides through a delegated chain with escalation.

Admin configures system (one-time + ongoing) | v Land Analyst uploads AFE or confirms email auto-ingestion | v System parses AFE (vision-language model) + auto-validates | +-----------------------+-----------------------+ | | | v v | [Land Analyst] [Technical Analyst] | Validates DOI Builds type curve + opex | Signs off w/ Signs off w/ | confidence confidence | | | | +-----------+-----------+ | | | v | Economics Engine auto-calculates full model | | | v | Commercial Analyst validates + adjusts | Signs off w/ confidence | | | v | Dashboard published (live interactive) | | | v | CAG reviews, changes inputs, votes (5-point) | | | v | Approver: Approve / Decline / Send Back | Delegated chain + time-based escalation | | | No action ---> Non-Consent flag |

Cross-Cutting Workflow Behaviors (v2.1):

  • Deadline-Aware Prioritization: All views sorted by election deadline urgency. Color-coded: green (>14 days), amber (7–14 days), red (<3 days). Proactive alerts when deadlines are at risk.
  • Confidence Auto-Gating: If any analyst signs off with Low confidence, the system auto-flags the evaluation to the Approver requiring explicit acknowledgment of the uncertainty.
  • Stale Evaluation Detection: Evaluations with no activity for a configurable period are flagged as stale. Admin and assigned analyst are notified; auto-escalation if deadline is approaching.
1
AFE Ingestion & Land Validation
Land Analyst Parallel with Stage 2

Trigger: AFE arrives via email auto-ingestion (AI agent detects, Land Analyst confirms) or manual PDF upload. ~50 operators, 3–5 AFEs/month.

Automated Steps

  1. Vision-language model parses AFE PDF to extract: API number, well name, location, stated interest, operator, costs
  2. Auto-validates against internal DOI deck (loaded from master Excel)
  3. Cross-references DOI against Enverus records (imported via CSV)
  4. Flags discrepancies with specific detail and severity

Manual Review

  • Land Analyst reviews matches, investigates discrepancies
  • Can override system-matched values with explanation
  • Signs off with confidence: High Medium or Low
OUTPUT: Validated WI, NRI, and leasehold burdens → Economics Engine
2
Technical Evaluation
Technical Analyst Parallel with Stage 1

Trigger: AFE ingestion (runs concurrently with Land Validation).

Automated Steps

  1. Search Enverus CSV data for analog wells by proximity, formation, well type, completion, operator, vintage
  2. Score and rank analogs; select top-N; retrieve production histories
  3. Generate type curve: normalize to time-zero, fit Arps decline, produce P10/P50/P90
  4. Generate monthly production forecast from projected spud date

Manual Review

  • Review analog selection; add/remove analogs with real-time type curve update
  • Review and adjust opex assumptions (fixed + variable, based on operator/basin)
  • Signs off with confidence: High Medium or Low
OUTPUT: Production forecast (P10/P50/P90) + opex assumptions → Economics Engine
3
Engine-Driven Economic Analysis
Economics Engine + Commercial Analyst

Trigger: Both Land and Technical sign-offs complete.

Engine Auto-Calculates

  1. Pulls type curve from TA, DOI/NRI from Land, capex from AFE, price deck from settings, opex from TA/benchmarks
  2. Computes: gross revenue → NRI → deal structure → net revenue → opex → EBITDA → capex → FCF → cumulative CF
  3. Produces DCF, NPV, IRR, payout, ROI, PI — full first-pass model with zero manual input

Commercial Analyst Validates & Adjusts

  • Reviews auto-generated model; adjusts price, opex, discount rate, deal structure
  • Configures deal structure: standard WI/NRI, back-in, sliding scale, min royalty, promoted, custom
  • Signs off with confidence: High Medium or Low
OUTPUT: Full economic model + dashboard-ready data → Comprehensive Deal Dashboard
4
CAG Review & Voting
Commercial Advisory Group

Trigger: Commercial Analyst sign-off publishes the comprehensive deal dashboard.

Live Interactive Dashboard

  • Production Profile Chart: Type curve decline (oil + gas)
  • Revenue Waterfall: Gross → royalty burden → ORRI → other burdens → net revenue
  • Full Cash Flow Analysis: All components in both tabular AND chart formats
  • Economics Summary: NPV, IRR, Payout, ROI, PI
  • Recommendation with Rationale: Explains WHY (not just a banner)
  • Analyst Confidence Indicators: High/Medium/Low from each stage
  • Change any input → everything recalculates live (including recommendation)

Voting

  • 5-point scale: Drill / Lean Drill / Neutral / Lean Drop / Drop
  • Mandatory rationale (at least one line per vote)
  • Sentiment summary: “4 of 6 advisors favor Drill”
  • All votes and rationale visible to Approver
OUTPUT: CAG votes + sentiment → Approver
5
Approval & Escalation
Approver (CEO + Delegates)

Trigger: CAG voting complete (or Approver can act early).

Approval Actions

  • Approve (Drill): Accept AFE, commit capital, archive with full context
  • Decline (Drop): Reject AFE, archive with full context
  • Send Back: Route to specific analyst with written questions

Delegated Approval Chain

  • Admin configures: Primary Approver → 1st Delegate → 2nd Delegate
  • Time-based escalation: configurable days before election deadline
  • Auto-escalates with notifications (in-app, email, SMS)
  • Any person can manually delegate via app or SMS (“Reply DELEGATE to pass to [Name]”)
  • If nobody acts before deadline → Non-Consent flag
FINAL: Decision archived with complete audit trail (votes, confidence, escalation history)

Data Strategy (v2.1): Free state regulatory data serves as the primary foundation for well and production data. State Regulatory Data Sources New in v2.1

Bulk file downloads • No API

Production data (monthly CSV/ASCII), well data (weekly ASCII), drilling permits, completions, field data, shapefiles. Covers Permian (Midland), Eagle Ford, TX-side Haynesville. Scheduled bulk download with incremental updates.

New Mexico — Oil Conservation Division (OCD)
Free REST API via OCD Hub (ArcGIS) • api.emnrd.nm.gov

Oil & gas wells, production statistics, injection totals. JSON, CSV, GeoJSON formats. Also: OCD Well Search (completions, formation tops, casing), NM Tech Octane Database. Covers Permian (Delaware). Direct API integration.

Wyoming — Oil & Gas Conservation Commission (WOGCC)
Free REST API via Data Explorer

Well info, location, status, API numbers, permits, production. CSV, GeoJSON, JSON. Wyoming Geospatial Hub for bulk fallback. Covers Powder River Basin. Direct API integration.

Louisiana — SONRIS
No reliable public API • Enverus CSV fallback

No clear programmatic access. Web-based reports only. ArcGIS MapServer has expired SSL cert. Covers Haynesville (LA side). Enverus CSV is the primary data path for Louisiana. Contact LA DNR for future bulk access.

Asymmetric Risk Framing v2.1

The recommendation section includes an asymmetric risk analysis panel:

  • Upside case (P10): NPV, IRR, and payout if the well outperforms
  • Base case (P50): Expected scenario economics
  • Downside case (P90): NPV, IRR, and payout if the well underperforms
  • Maximum loss: Capex exposure if the well is a total dry hole
Well Performs (P10–P50)Well Underperforms (P90)
DrillUpside captured (NPV at P10)Downside realized (NPV at P90)
DropOpportunity cost forfeitedLoss avoided

This regret analysis shifts the decision from “is this a good well?” to “is the risk-reward acceptable?” All values update live with other dashboard components.

10. Decision Archive & Analytics

Decision Record Contents

  • AFE identifier (API number, well name, operator)
  • Evaluation date range (received to decision)
  • Land validation results, confidence level, and sign-off timestamp
  • Analog wells, type curve parameters, opex assumptions, technical confidence level, sign-off
  • Economic model inputs/outputs, deal structure config, commercial confidence level, sign-off
  • Auto-generated model (before Commercial Analyst adjustments) — preserved for audit
  • System-generated recommendation with rationale
  • CAG votes: Each member's vote (5-point scale), rationale, and timestamp
  • CAG sentiment summary: Aggregated vote distribution
  • Final decision: Approve (Drill) / Decline (Drop) / Non-Consent
  • Send Back history: Questions to analysts, responses, re-sign-off records
  • Override annotation (if applicable)
  • Approval chain activity: Notifications, escalations, delegation history
  • Non-consent record: Penalty data from DOI deck, contributing factors
  • All scenarios explored during the evaluation

Search & Filter

  • By operator, basin, formation, date range, decision outcome, non-consent status
  • By economic metrics (e.g., “NPV > $1M”)
  • By analyst confidence level (e.g., “any analyst flagged Low”)
  • By CAG sentiment (e.g., “majority voted Lean Drop or Drop”)

11. Technical Considerations

11.1 Enverus Integration

Enrichment & Fallback: CSV Import. With state regulatory data as the primary foundation, Enverus CSV serves as enrichment (completions detail, DOI cross-referencing, directional surveys) and primary data path for Louisiana/Haynesville. Configurable column mapping, data validation, incremental import with duplicate detection by API number.

Future: Enverus Developer API v3 to supplement both state data and CSV. Subscription and cost verification required.

11.2 State Regulatory Data Integration New in v2.1

Free state regulatory data serves as the primary foundation. Each state requires a different integration approach:

11.3 PDF Parsing for AFE Documents

Hybrid approach: Vision-language model (GPT-4 Vision or Claude) for prompt-based field extraction. Handles layout variability across ~50 operators without custom training. Manual correction fallback. Cloud-hosted — no data sensitivity constraints.

11.4 Email Auto-Ingestion New in v2.0

Connect to shared inbox via IMAP, Microsoft Graph API, or Google Workspace API. Rule-based classification (sender domain, subject keywords, PDF attachment) supplemented by AI. Detected AFEs queued in “pending confirmation” state. Land Analyst confirms to trigger parsing. False positives dismissible.

11.5 Commodity Pricing

MVP: Fixed Price Deck. User sets WTI and HH prices (flat or escalation), basin-specific differentials. Stored per evaluation.

Future: EIA Open Data API + CME settlement data for live pricing.

11.6 Public Opex Benchmarking

Curated baseline from SEC filings. Lookup: operator + basin + year → LOE/BOE. Technical Analyst provides per-evaluation assumptions. Manual override. Illustrative ranges: Permian $6–12/BOE, Eagle Ford $7–13/BOE, Haynesville $5–10/BOE, Powder River $8–14/BOE.

11.7 Type Curve Methodology

Select analogs → normalize to time-zero → fit modified hyperbolic (Arps) → P10/P50/P90 → extend to economic limit. Robust curve fitting, multi-phase handling (oil + gas + water), anomaly filtering.

11.8 SMS Integration New in v2.0

Cloud SMS gateway (Twilio or equivalent). Outbound: escalation alerts, deadline warnings. Inbound: “DELEGATE” and “APPROVE”/“DECLINE” commands with confirmation step. All SMS interactions logged in audit trail.

16.1 Weighted Confidence Auto-Gating Approved v1

If any analyst signs off with Low confidence, the system auto-flags the evaluation to the Approver requiring explicit acknowledgment. The Approver must annotate acceptance of the risk. Transforms confidence from passive indicator into active workflow gate.

16.2 Deadline-Aware Prioritization Approved v1

All views sorted by election deadline urgency. Color-coded: green (>14 days), amber (7–14 days), red (<3 days). Proactive alerts when deadlines are at risk based on processing time estimates.

16.4 Breakeven Auto-Calculation Approved v1

Auto-compute breakeven oil price, gas price, capex, opex, and minimum WI (NPV=0). Displayed on dashboard with “margin of safety” framing in recommendation rationale.

16.6 Asymmetric Risk Framing Approved v1

P10 upside vs P90 downside with regret analysis. 2x2 decision matrix (Drill vs. Drop at P10 vs. P90). Maximum loss (dry hole capex exposure). All values update live. Shifts decision to “is the risk-reward acceptable?”

16.9 Stale Evaluation Detection Approved v1

Flag evaluations with no activity for configurable period. Notify Admin and assigned analyst. Auto-escalate if deadline approaching. Track stale metrics in admin views.

16.10 Decision Outcome Feedback Loop Approved v1 (data model only)

For every “Drill” decision, create pending Outcome Record. Import mechanism for actual production data (state data + Enverus CSV). Auto-match by API number. Status tags. No variance UI in v1 — data collection only to seed v2 analytics.

16.11 PDF Template Library Approved v1

Cache extraction mappings after successful parses. Template-first for repeat operators (fast, cheap, deterministic). Vision-language model fallback for new operators. Reduces AI API costs over time as library grows across ~50 operators.

16.12 Export Package Approved v1

One-click: PDF report (all dashboard components), Excel workbook (full economic model), executive summary slide (board-ready). Reflects current dashboard state. Standard deliverable for JV partners and board communication.

Approved for v2 (1 item — data model in v1)

16.5 Operator Performance Scorecards Approved v2

Full scorecard UI in v2. Data model designed in v1: operator entity with performance tracking fields (EUR, IP-30, IP-90, completion consistency, DOI accuracy, AFE history). Data accumulates automatically as evaluations are processed.

Deferred (2 items)

16.7 Analyst Workload Dashboard Deferred

Analyst roles may evolve or be reduced as state data integrations and automation become more seamless. Revisit when role structure stabilizes post-MVP.

16.8 CAG Vote Weighting Deferred

Equal votes in MVP. Approver uses their own judgment to weight advisory input by expertise. May revisit in v2 if CAG grows large enough to warrant formal weighting.

Skipped (1 item)

16.3 Quick Take Pre-Screen Skipped

Client decision: every AFE runs the full evaluation process. No fast-track or pre-screening.
